President Ho Chi Minh

The man who saved millions of Vietnamese' lives

Here's a time line of President Ho Chi Minh's life:

  • 1890 - Born in Nam Dan, Nghe An.
  • 1911 - Leaves school to go abroad.
  • 1917 - The success of the Russian October Revolution brings him to Marxism–Leninism.
  • 1919 - Joins France Socialist Party and works in the Labour Movement.
  • 1920 - Becomes a founding member of the French Communist Party and is an outspoken leader of the Vietnamese community in Paris, participating in the Intercolonial Union formed under Communist sponsorship and publishing two anticolonial journals.
  • 1923 - Is invited to Moscow, Russia, where he studies at the University of Oriental Workers.
  • 1925 - Is sent to China to organize a communist movement and forms the Thanh Nien (Vietnamese Revolutionary Youth League).
  • 1930 - Unites the factions as a formal Communist Party, drawing its members from Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos.
  • 1940 - Announces the formation of the Viet Minh (League for Vietnamese Independence), an organization demanding independence from French rule and Japanese military occupation.
  • 1941 - Leads the ICP in seeking support for the Viet Minh, forming alliances with American diplomats and intelligence officers in South China, helping victims of a famine that killed over two million people in north and central Vietnam from 1943 to 1944, and building up the party's military forces.
  • 1945 - As the president of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam, standing before thousands of supporters in the city of Hanoi, he proclaims "that Vietnam has the right to be a free and independent country — and in fact is so already.
  • 1954 - Remains president of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and chairman of the Communist Party but slowly turns over day-to-day responsibilities to others.
  • 1956 - Fails in a land reform campaign.
  • 1960 - Oversees the formation of the National Liberation Front (NLF), a movement of resistance against the non-Communist government in southern Vietnam.
  • 1965 - Maintains good relations with the Soviet Union and China in order to obtain military assistance and supplies from both Communist powers. His health slowly declines.
  • 1969 - Dies of a heart attack at the age of 79.
